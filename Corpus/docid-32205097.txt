‘MEMORANDUM FOR THE PRESIDENT

SUBJECT: Your meeting with the Foreign Intelligence Advisory
Board at 4:00 PM today

Mr. Clark Clifford, who has been Chairman of the President's
Foreign Intelligence Advisory Board since April 1963, will introduce
his Board members and explain the work of the Board. He is
expected to emphasize the Board's recommendations on how to im-
prove counterintelligence activities, including suggestions as to
revision of the existing procedures used to prevent the Russians
from penetrating our intelligence organizations.

Since 1946, there has been a group under various names to
advise the President as to intelligence problems. The current
group was asked by President Kennedy to dig deeply into how the
intelligence agencies operated in the Cuba situation. The Board's
general charter includes the xim review and assessment of the
objectives and the conduct of all our foreign intelligence activities.
It is advisory to you and is in a position to make judgments as to
how well our foreign intelligence work is being done, independent

of judgments made by Director McCone and other intelligence officers.
