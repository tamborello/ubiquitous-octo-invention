MEMORANDUM FOR THE PRESIDENT

SUBJECT: Your meeting with the President's Foreign Intelligence
Advisory Board at 4:00 PM today

Mr. Clark Clifford, who has been Chairman of the Foreign Intelligence
Advisory Board since April 1963, will introduce his Board members
and explain the work of the Board.

Mr. Clifford plans to summarize the Board's recommendations on
how te improve counterintelligence activities, including proposed
revisions of the existing procedures aimed at preventing the Russians
from penetrating our intelligence organizetions.

The group is advisory to you and you can use it ag you see fit. One
of the major tasks it undertock for President Kennedy was to dig
deeply into how the intelligence agencies operated in the Cuba situa-
tion, The Board is in a position to make independent judgmentsfor
you as to how well our foreign intelligence work is being done.

Suggested questions:

i. Would the Board report to me on how the intelligence
organizations, especially the military agencies, are re~
flecting my current instructions to economize and hold
down the number of government employees ?

2. Would the Board suggest te me ways of speeding up the
work which ie now going on aimed at consolidating intelli-
gence activities and anding overlapping activities 7

3. Does the Board have a view as to whether Congress
needs to know more about our intelligence activities than
is now provided to the appropriate committees ?

Brovraley Smith
