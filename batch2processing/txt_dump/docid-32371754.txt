4, Operation Ss.

‘a. On13 and 14 January 1964, Phineas F. SLINKARD requested PARMUT
to shoot movies of two BEDOX targets who were scheduled to meet at one of the
target apartments at 1500 hours on the above mentioned dates, On 13 January,

' one of the targets telephoned and said he could not make it but would be there the
following day. (LIFEAT coverage). On 14 January, the target approached the
apartment building, rang the bell, and immediately left the building entrance and
walked down a block, giving his counter-part time to walk down three flights of
stairs to open the door for him, LIFIRE truck coverage did photograph the
Subject ringing the bell and leaving, but could not photograph the Subject walking
away because o obstructing traffic. The target was also photographed running
through the door which was opened by another occupant of the apartment building.
To provide photographic and evidential confirmation of the date of this meeting,
RESCIGNO walked to the entrance of the apartment and read a local newspaper
which was also photographed, Three rolls of Tri-x negative film were used on
this operation. Forwarded as enclosure 2 is a copy of the film for Headquarters
retention.

b. On the-early morning of 21 January 1964, PARMUTH and
RESCIGNO were requested by Lee R. MYLECHRAINE to photograph a meeting
between Oliver Q. WIMPHEN and a target who is a leader of a dissident factor
of the Mexican Communist Party. An approach was made by WIMPHEN but he
could not guide the target to where this meeting could be photographed by the
LIFIRE truck.
