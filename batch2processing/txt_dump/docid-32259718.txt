MEMORANDUM FOR THE DIRECTOR

FROM: BETSY PALMER’ ($P.

DATE: OCTOBER 5, 1978

SUBJECT: FBI File Review - Cuba Libre
On October 5, 1978, I reviewed the four volume file on

Cuba Libre. The first three volumes related to publications
entitled, Cuba Libre, one pro-Castro published in New York
under the direction of Wilfredo Alfonso, and one anti-Castro
published in Miami published by Jose Acosta. .

Volume 4 contained information about the Cuban exile organi-
zation named Cuba Libre and pertinent notes on this group
are outlined below.

Volume 4 - Various See References

 

1. #2-1787-17
Report from Miami
April 17, 1963
Title: Possible Anti-Castro Raid on Cuba Scheduled
for About December 16, 1962 - Nettrality Matters

Roberto Parson Ramirez and Antonio Gutillo have a small
group headquartered in Miami called Cuba Libre. They
had contacted representatives of the U.S. Government
requesting financial aid but have. not received word that
any will be forthcoming. Cuba Libra not expecting any
U.S. Government support. Cuba Libra has no money, arms,
training camp or transportation. They have no meetings
and engage in no propaganda or publicity.

2. #105-134373-20
Report from Miami
August 6, 1965
Title: Carlos Zarraga Martinez - Internal Security, Cuba

Informant said on June 17, 1965 that Zarraga continues as
head of small, independent, anti-Castro organization,

Cuba Libre. The group recently acquired a motor boat

and plans to send commandos into Cuba. The Boat was made
available by RECE. When RECE announced a union with the
MIRR and Commandos L on June 22, 1965, Zarraga was ready

to return the boat, because he did not wish to be associated
in any way with MIRR. He met with Polo Miranda of RECE

who convinced him to keep the boat.
