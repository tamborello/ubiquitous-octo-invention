Pittsburgh to Las Vegas permanently and JAFFEE planned

to sell Rapid Tag and Wire Co., Pittsburgh, of which he. was
President, but would continue to. hold a share of the Edison
Hotel in Pittsburgh. Informant stated that JAFFEE, in
Pittsburgh, had the reputation of being a horse player and
gambler and that he had never been in trouble with the police.

on 2/7/58 former PG 509-C advised that MILTON JAFFEE of
Pittsburgh, together with JOSEPH EPSTEIN of Chicago and
MORRIS KLEINMAN of Cleveland, Ohio, were partners in the
Stardust Hotel and gambling casino being built in Las Vegas.
Informant stated that JAFFEE had over the years made it
clear that he wanted no part of any gambler who had a
criminal record or who was allied with what he called the
syndicate; that JAFFEE tried to maintain friendly relations
with persons connected with the syndicate, but that he
refused to associate himself with them in any form of
business; and that JAFFEE was interested in the Stardust
Hotel with the same. group, which, back in 1953 was
interested in the Last Frontier Hotel.
