Title: John F. Kennedy Records Collection
, Act of 1992 (the Act)

Synopsis: The Act established the Assassination Records Review
Board (the Board) to over see the governments'! production and
compliance with the Act. The Board reviews the proposed
postponements in documents sent by each agency to the National
Archives and Records Administration (NARA) decides what
postponements the Act allows or opens the document in full. The
Board's responsibilities include having agencies search other
subjects’ and areas to find any additional material not already
identified as assassination records. The establishment of the
Board by the Act also limited the time for their duties to be
accomplished. The Board must complete their tasks by September
1997. With this deadline in mind, the Board sent letter
requesting additional information.

Details: On November 29, 1996, the Board sent a letter to the

FBI setting forth a formal compliance program with stated
deadlines. The Board's goal is to assure Congress and the :
American public that all was done to accomplish the intentions of
the Act. The letter requested the FBI to designate an “Agency
Compliance Official” and prepare a “Statement of Compliance”.
Members of the JFK Task Force and the Board's staff met on
January 15, 1997, to clarify the Board's expectations of the FBI
considering the November letter. -
