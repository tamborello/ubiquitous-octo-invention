Abolish the House Un-American Activities Committee. This list
included the name Professor DERK BODDE, University of Pennsylvania.

An article appeared in "The Daily Pennsylvanian,"
a student newspaper published at the University of Pennsylvania,
in the edition dated 1/24/63, which purported to be a portrait
of Dr. BODDE. This article said that. DERK BODDE was one of
the country's foremost Sinologists and went on to say that he
was a champion of liberal causes, was one of the "peacemongers"
who petitioned the President over Cuba last fall, favors
recommendation of Communist China and feels that Communist
China should be admitted to the United Nations.

On 9/12/60, EUGENE T, LYDDONE, Deputy Clerk of the
Supreme Court, made available to SA O. MICHAEL SMASAL a brief
for ‘Amici Curiae, which indicates the Communist Party of the
United States, Petitioner, v. Subversive Activities Control
Board, Recipient. In Appendix A there was set out the names
and addresses by city and state of these Amici Curiae. Pro-
fessor DERK BODDE was one of those so listed.

On 9/30/60, Dr. ARLEIGH P, HESS, JR., Director,
College of General Studies, University of Pennsylvania,
advised SA EDWARD A, SMITH that DERK BODDE was one of those
who attended the International Congress of Orientalists in
Moscow in August 1960.
